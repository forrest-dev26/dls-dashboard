# Eleanor Ad Prompt Template — v1 (eleanor-v1)

## Avatar

Eleanor — primary buyer (~60% of orders). 52, married, two kids in college or just out.
Works in healthcare administration, education, nonprofit, or middle management.
Lives in Charlotte, Raleigh, suburban Boston, Madison, Boise, or somewhere comparable.

**Why she buys:** Wants something that respects her intelligence and gives her a small thing
to look forward to. The physical mail is the point — she is tired of screens and likes the
deliberateness of it.

**What she reads:** Tana French, Kate Atkinson, Anthony Horowitz, Erik Larson, Hilary Mantel
for ambitious days. Subscribes to The Atlantic or The New Yorker even if she doesn't always
finish them.

**How she found you:** Instagram or Facebook ad, podcast mention, friend's recommendation.
Possibly a gift she received that she then bought again for herself.

**What she will pay:** $199 without flinching. $249 if framing is right. Not price-sensitive
in the way younger buyers are — time-sensitive and quality-sensitive.

## Voice Rules

### DO
- Lean into respect-for-intelligence framing. She doesn't want to be sold to; she wants to be invited to something good.
- Anti-screen / pro-physical / pro-deliberate is a recurring hook.
- "Something to look forward to" is the emotional engine — anticipation, not adrenaline.
- Imagery: warm, atmospheric, period-accurate to the series. Slightly cinematic but never cartoonish.
- Copy: literate, confident, never breathless. Em-dashes welcome. Sentences can be short or long but never juvenile.

### DO NOT
- No cliched Halloween imagery for Salem (no jack-o-lanterns, no cackling witches, no green skin).
- No shipwreck-disaster imagery for Titanic (no sinking ship, no iceberg crash). It's a story aboard the ship, not a disaster movie.
- No straitjackets or institutional-horror imagery for Asylum (it's gothic mystery, not exploitation).
- No "you won't believe..." breathless copy. No exclamation marks in body copy.
- No "subscribers" — say "members."
- No wax seals, no handwritten claims, no "inserts," no "printed cards."
- No emojis in body copy.
- No fake urgency ("Last chance!", "Only 50 left!").

## Series Color Palettes

- **Salem (1692):** candlelight, deep brown, dusk
- **Titanic (1912):** navy, brass, pale gold, soft fog
- **Asylum (1920s):** jade, cream, sepia, art-deco accents
